Guide to simple estradiol gel

Materials

Reusable

  • Digital scale, at least 0.001g precision
  • Rapid Stirrer
    • Can be either a lab-style magnetic stirrer or a food-grade milk frother
  • Dispenser
    • Airless cosmetic pumps are ideal, although other may be effective

Consumables

  • Estradiol
    • For more details, see sourcing
  • Hand sanitizer
    • Make sure to get an alcohol-based sanitizer with no denaturing agents.
    • Ideally in the 60%-70% alcohol range
  • Solvent
    • Can use either isopropyl alcohol or ethanol (everclear), the higher the purity the better
  • Distilled Water
    • Could probably use tap water in a pinch, but better safe than sorry

 

Procedure

(photo guide https://imgur.com/a/Tn6XIxa)

Excel Sheet for math

 

  1. Calibrate/Test your scale. Exact procedure for this varies scale to scale, check your manual
  2. Measure out you hand sanitizer. For this guide we're using 120g, but you can scale up or down
  3. Measure out your solvent. We're using 15g 
    • For both of these measurements, these numbers are approximate targets. You numbers can vary Â±10%, but make sure to use your exact numbers when doing the math for later steps
  4. Calculate the water required to bring the final solution to 63% alcohol. The excel sheet makes this easy, but you could do it by hand
  5. Measure the water and add it to the hand sanitizer
  6. Calculate the desired amount of E 
    • This method should work with 0.6% to 0.12%. This guide targeting 0.1%
    • Again, the excel sheet makes the math easier
  7. Measure out your E
    • This is often the hardest step, since it's measuring both a powder not a liquid, and a very small amount. 
    • Go slow, and don't be afraid to start this step over
  8. Add the E to the solvent
  9. (Optional) add a drop of food coloring to the solvent. 
  10. Mix the E and solvent until the E is fully dissolved. 
    • You probably don't need the stirrer for this one, just shake until dissolved
  11. Mix the solvent into the hand sanitizer
    • Use the stirrer for this one
  12. Pour the fully mixed gel into your dispenser(s)
  13. Measure your dispenser's output
    • Best way I've found it to just measure out 10-20 pumps onto your scale, and take the average
  14. Calculate dosing 
    1. The basic way to do this is E target / (gel per pump * E concentration)
    2. So in my scenario, I want 2mg, I have a 0.1% concentration, and my pump puts out 0.22g, so I'd do 2mg / (220mg * 0.001) which works out to 9 pumps
    3. This is also in the Excel sheet if you don't like math
  15. Enjoy :)
