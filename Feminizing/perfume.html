Perfume based spray

Many perfumes are ethanol with scents dissolved in them. It should be possible to also dissolve estradiol into the spray, making something similar to the Lenzetto spray.

Similar to the scratch spray, you should also use a penetration enhancer like octisalate

 

Production

Since perfumes are not uniform in production or spray volume, we can't give universal directions. This will just be general guidance

  • You can't go more than 20mg estradiol per 1mL of ethanol
    • To figure out how much you need, look at how much your dispenser sprays and what your dose is
    • You should aim to get your whole dose in 1-2 sprays
  • Octisalate should be 5x your estradiol, by mass
  • 63% ethanol is ideal, but it's a fairly shallow drop off so 60%-70% should be ok. 50%-80% would likely still work, but at reduced effectiveness.

 

Mix together your perfume, estradiol, octisalate, and distilled water as needed.

 

Use

Straightforward, spray on skin and leave that section alone until it drys. Note that additional successive sprays on the same patch of skin doesn't linearly increase dosage.1 That means if you need more than one spray you should either mix a higher strength spray or use 2 patches of skin (ie both arms). It seems it's best to wait at least 6 hours between spraying the same spot
